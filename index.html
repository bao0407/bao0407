Simulation of Starlink LEO Satellites with ns-3 and Optional DDoS Vulnerability Assessment

Abstract

Starlink, a satellite internet constellation developed by SpaceX, aims to provide high-speed internet access across the globe. This constellation consists of numerous low Earth orbit (LEO) satellites. However, the complexity of such a network poses significant challenges in terms of performance, reliability, and security. A simulation-based approach is crucial for understanding and optimizing these aspects before actual deployment. This project proposes to simulate Starlink's LEO satellite network using the network simulator ns-3 to evaluate its performance under various conditions. Additionally, we aim to assess the network's vulnerability to Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attacks, an increasingly relevant concern in satellite communication networks.
